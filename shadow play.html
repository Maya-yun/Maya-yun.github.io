The design of Shadow play

Shadow Puppets, also known as "Shadow Puppets," or "lantern Puppets," are folk plays in which people are cut out of animal skins or cardboard pieces to perform stories. Behind a white curtain, the performers manipulate the characters while telling stories in popular local tunes, accompanied by percussion instruments and strings that have a strong local flavor. Its popular range is very extensive, and because of the different vocal cavity performed in different parts of the formation of a variety of shadow puppetry.Shadow puppetry is an ancient folk art in China. Old beijingers call it "donkey shadow play". According to historical records, shadow puppetry began in the western han dynasty, flourished in the tang dynasty, and flourished in the qing dynasty.

"Shadow puppetry" is a general term for shadow puppetry and its characters (including scenes, props and objects). Shadow puppetry is a theatrical form that allows the audience to watch a kind of light shadow performed by a planar figure through a white curtain to achieve artistic effect. However, in shadow puppetry, the plane figures and scenes are usually made of leather products made by folk artists by hand, carving and painting with knives, so it is called shadow puppetry. Shadow puppetry used to be one of the most popular folk entertainments in the old days before movies and television.

China is a vast country, and shadow puppets from all over the country have their own characteristics. However, most of the production procedures of shadow puppets are the same. Usually, they have to go through eight processes, such as skin selection, skin making, drawing, overdrawing, carving, color application, sweating and ironing, and composing. The artistic creativity of shadow puppetry draws on the techniques and styles of silk paintings, stone paintings and brick paintings of the han dynasty and mural paintings of temples in the tang and song dynasties.

The limbs and head of the shadow puppets are carved separately and stitched together so that they can move freely during the performance. A shadow puppeteer, to use five bamboo stick manipulation, the artist's fingers are flexible, often play the audience dazzling. Not only the hands are excellent, but also the mouth to say, read, play, sing, and at the foot of the brake gongs and drums. The screen of shadow play is made of a piece of white gauze of 1 square meter size. The white gauze becomes crisp and clear after being polished with fish oil. During the performance, shadow puppets move close to the screen, with vivid figures and colorful colors. Shadow puppetry is popular among the people because of its small props, convenient performance, free from venue restrictions and no formal training for actors. In areas where shadow puppetry is popular, it is affectionately known as the art of "picking".
